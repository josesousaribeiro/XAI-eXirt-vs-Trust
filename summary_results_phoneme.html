Performance Stability Analysis

The initial analysis of the pipeline focuses on the performance stability of the models under both baseline and perturbed conditions. Table 1 presents the evaluation metrics — Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-score, and ROC AUC — for the four models across different levels of data perturbation.

Table 1: Summary of the model’s performances, according to tests carried out.

Figure 1 presents the pairwise p-values derived from the Friedman-Nemenyi test, comparing the performance metrics reported in Table 1. Within the comparison matrix, lower p-values indicate stronger evidence to reject the null hypothesis of performance equivalence. Adopting a significance threshold of alpha = 0.05, we can identify statistically significant differences with 95% confidence.

Identification of reliable models

Figures 5 and 6 were generated exclusively using the eXirt method. The purpose of this analysis is to validate the method ability to extract experimental metadata grounded in Item Response Theory (IRT), allowing for a clear distinction between inherently reliable predictive models and those operating as subproducts (artifacts) of perturbed data. This approach enables the diagnosis of classifier behavior under stress, utilizing item characteristic curves to measure the level of uncertainty introduced by data perturbations.
