Results obtained from applying the experimental pipeline to four binary classification datasets, as described in the article "Reliability in XAI: Do Model Explanations Depend on Data or Algorithms?".

About this summary

In paper "Reliability in XAI: Do Model Explanations Depend on Data or Algorithms?" the experimental evaluations were conducted using four representative real-world binary classification datasets: diabetes (9 attributes and 768 instances), banknote-authentication (5 attributes and 1,372 instances), phoneme (6 attributes and 5,404 instances), and mozilla4 (6 attributes and 15,545 instances). The selection of these datasets enabled the validation of the pipeline effectiveness across various domains and data distributions, ensuring that the observations regarding stability and reliability reflect practical, real-world challenges.
